Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the NextLeap UI/UX Designer Fellowship?

    Our UI/UX Designer Fellowship is a 16 week online course that helps learners transition into a career in UI/UX design in tech companies The entire fellowship is taught online via live sessions on Zoom. Our instructors and mentors are design leaders at top technology companies and they will not only teach you the fundamentals of product design and UI/UX but also help you with case studies, doubt resolution and feedback on your design portfolios. . So, if you are looking to transition into UX design roles and are willing to devote the required time and effort to pick up the skills, you have come to the right place.

  • What if I miss a live session?

    Recordings will be made available for all of the live sessions with instructors. Further, please note that we've designed our sessions to be participatory and interactive, and recommend that you attend all to make the most of them.

  • Who can enrol for these fellowships?

    The fellowships are open to anyone trying to break into a new role and there is no requirement for minimum experience. However, if you’re currently studying, please note that career services will not be available to you unless you’re a final year student.

  • I'm a pre-final year student. Am I eligible to apply?

    Yes, you can apply for the program. However, you won’t be eligible to access our career services.

  • What is the graduation project?

    At the end of the fellowship we will release a problem statement for you to work. Your submission to this problem statement is called the graduation project.

  • Can I re-submit my graduation project?

    We will release a new problem statement at the start of every month. Once you have completed the fellowship, you will get 3 attempts in the next 3 consecutive months (after the fellowship) to submit your graduation project.

  • What's the problem statement that I'll be required to work on for the graduation project?

    A problem statement will be released at the start of the first month after you have completed the fellowship. Thereafter, you'll have a time period of ~1 month to submit your graduation project.

  • Who all will be eligible to get access to career services?

    You need to submit your graduation project and score more than 70% in your project to get access to our career services.

  • What’s included in the career services?

    As part of our career services, we’ll make your profile available to our partner hiring companies. Depending on their requirement and selection criteria they will reach out to you for interviews. Learners from our previous cohorts have gotten hired at companies such as OLA, PhonePe, Slice, Cars24, Media.net, Swiggy and many more.

  • If I don’t score more than 70%, will I still get a completion certificate?

    Completion certificate will be given to every learner who submits the graduation project irrespective of whether you make the cut-off or not.

  • Is work experience also a criterion to get selected for interviews?

    No, not all! No prior experience is required. You need to complete your weekly milestones and score 70% on your graduation project to be eligible to appear for interviews.

  • Are the programs open to Postgraduate students? Are they open to people with non-tech backgrounds? Is there a criterion for CGPA?

    College/degree/GPA does not matter. Only your learning mindset and demonstration of learning via the submissions matter

  • Will the interviews be for internships or full time roles?

    The interviews will be conducted for both full time roles and internships.

  • Which companies are hiring?

    There have been 500+ hiring partners so far. A lot of companies have already signed up for the new cohorts in each domain and the final list of the hiring partners will be shared upon commencement of the respective Fellowships.

  • If I am unable to attend the program or need to pull out at the last minute, will I get a refund?

    Refund requests can be raised only before the 28th of the month preceding the Fellowship start date. If the reason is found genuine, we'll refund 100% of the paid amount. Any requests raised after the aforementioned timeline will not be processed. The decision on refund requests will be at the discretion of the NextLeap team.

  • Can I get this reimbursed from my company?

    We provide a certificate of completion and an invoice as proof of your participation in this course. Our learners have found these documents to be sufficient to claim a reimbursement from their employers.

NextLeap UI/UX Designer Fellowship: Break into UI/UX Design with our 16 week UI/UX Design Course

The NextLeap UI/UX Designer Fellowship is the perfect launchpad for aspiring UI/UX designers looking to break into the industry. Our structured UI/UX design course offers a hands-on learning experience, equipping you with the skills, tools and insights required to become a successful UI/UX Designer or Product Designer in today's competitive landscape.

Who is it for

The NextLeap UI/UX Designer Fellowship is designed for early career working professionals looking to transition into UI/UX design roles like UI/UX Designer, UX Designer, Product Designer in top technology companies. Our average cohort has

  • 1-2 years of work experience, many in graphic design
  • 90% of the cohort is made up of working professionals and the remaining with college students.

While no prior design knowledge is required, we expect students to put in 14-15 hours of effort every week to master the relevant skills required for the role.

An industry relevant Curriculum

You will go through an intensive 16 week learning journey which will cover a comprehensive curriculum to help you master all the skills you need to design and build successful products in this new AI age.

Week 1: Business Thinking in Design

  • Systems Thinking : understanding actors, flows, bottlenecks
  • Systems Thinking Tools: behaviour over time graphs, causal diagrams, iceberg model
  • First principles thinking: actor, action, entity diagrams
  • Understanding different types of Business Models
  • Building KPI Trees for structured problem solving

Hands-on Learning:
Case on Swiggy Customer Support: Building a KPI Tree to map business outcomes to product outcomes

Week 2: Product Discovery & User Research

  • Impact Mapping to map product outcomes to opportunities
  • User segmentation methodologies
  • Opportunity sizing
  • User Research Methodologies
  • Identifying unmet needs and Jobs to be Done framework
  • Journey Mapping & their relevance
  • Building User Personas
  • Affinity Mapping to derive insights from user research
  • How to use AI tools for user research

Hands-on Learning:
Case on an early stage food-tech startup: Analysing a dataset to identify the target user segment and crafting a user research plan

Week 3: Problem Framing & Ideation

  • Hypotheses building
  • Problem Framing canvas
  • Mindmapping solutions
  • Understanding Benefits and constraints for each actor
  • Understanding product risks
  • Crafting User Stories
  • Understanding Human Motivations to build solutions
  • Prioritising between solutions
  • Understanding how to build user flows and wireframes

Hands-on Learning:
Case on building a new feature for Spotify: Build an AI assisted prototype using vibe coding tools

Week 4: UX Foundations & AI Prototyping

  • Learn about UX Laws
  • How to think about Information architecture in UX Design
  • How to design prototypes on Figma
  • Learn AI assisted prototyping with vibe coding tools
  • Spec driven development with vibe coding tools
  • Data model driven development with vibe coding tools

Hands-on Learning:
Figma Hours: Layers & Objects

Week 5: Introduction to Visual Design

  • Learn about the application of Gestalt’s Principles
  • Understand how Scale & Visual Hierarchy influence design
  • Learn the difference between Balance & Contrast and how to design keeping these in mind
  • Master the usage of Visual Design elements (lines, shapes, colours, grids, typography)

Hands-on Learning:
Figma Hours: Typography & Text Styling

Week 6: Visual Design Language

  • Learn Moodboarding strategies
  • Master Colour Theory and how to apply when designing products
  • Understand the principles and best practices of Typography settings in digital product design
  • Learn about grids & layouts

Hands-on Learning:
Figma Hours: Colours, gradients & styling

Week 7: Design Systems

  • Learn about Inclusive design practices and why they matter
  • Learn to build with Design Accessibility in mind
  • Learn how to build Atomic Design systems
  • Master Design Systems Tools and best practices
  • Learn to build design systems and component libraries with AI tools like Claude

Hands-on Learning:
Figma Hours: Component Libraries

Week 8: Android Material Design Deepdive

  • Components in Android Material Design

  • Navigation View

Work on real world projects: Apply what you learn

The 16-week UI/UX Designer Fellowship is a hands-on learning experience where you will work on real world projects and build a strong product portfolio to stand out from the competition.

  • Fortnightly assignments: Step into the shoes of a UI/UX Designer to solve a real-world problem by going through the design lifecycle. During the Fellowship, you will work on a set of 3 assignments – start with a breakdown of the problem, go deeper with user research, frame the problem, ideate possible solutions, craft wireframes, define metrics and a design specifications document.
  • Learn in Public Challenges: Go the extra mile by working on projects focused on designing on Figma by recreating/reimagining screens of popular apps.
  • AI Hackathons: Participate in monthly community hackathons to build prototypes, workflows and agents using AI tools.
  • Graduation Project: Put your skills to test with the graduation project by working on a real-world project that will be evaluated by our mentors.
